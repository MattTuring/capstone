Bitcoin: The Grandfather of Cryptocurreny

Bitcoin was invented by an unknown person or group of people using the name Satoshi Nakamoto and was released as open-source software in 2009. In 2010, the first known commercial transaction using bitcoin occurred when programmer Laszlo Hanyecz bought two Papa John's pizzas for â‚¿10,000 or about 205 Million USD as current valuation. The overwhelming majority of bitcoin transactions take place on a cryptocurrency exchange, rather than being used in transactions with merchants. Delays processing payments through the blockchain of about ten minutes make bitcoin use very difficult in a retail setting. Prices are not usually quoted in units of bitcoin and many trades involve one, or sometimes two, conversions into conventional currencies. Merchants that do accept bitcoin payments may use payment service providers to perform the conversions. The price of bitcoins has gone through cycles of appreciation and depreciation referred to by some as bubbles and busts.

Because of bitcoin's decentralized nature and its trading on online exchanges located in many countries, regulation of bitcoin has been difficult. However, the use of bitcoin can be criminalized.

Ethereum: King of the Dapps

Ethereum provides a decentralized virtual machine, the Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM), which can execute scripts. The virtual machine is what is used to run the DApps. Every transaction uses the native blockchain asset known as Ether. The other main difference between Bitcoin is that the supply of the chain asset is currently uncapped. There are plans to limit the supply in the future but no concrete action has been taken. The fees to use the network are determined by the complexitiy of the transaction.

Source


Ripple: The Banking Coin

Ripple has been adopted by banks and payment networks as settlement infrastructure technology. Ripple relies on a common shared ledger, which is a distributed database storing information about all Ripple accounts. The network is "managed by a network of independent validating servers that constantly compare their transaction records." Servers could belong to anyone, including banks or market makers. Ripple validates accounts and balances instantly for payment transmission and delivers payment notification with very little latency (within a few seconds). Payments are irreversible, and there are no chargebacks.

A unique feature is that the total supply of the currecny decreases with each use.
